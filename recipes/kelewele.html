KELEWELE

INGREDIENTS

  • Plantains: 3 to 5 very ripe plantains (yellow with black spots).
  • Aromatics: 1 small onion, a 2–3 inch piece of fresh ginger, and 1–2 scotch bonnet peppers (adjust for heat).
  • Spices: 1/2 teaspoon salt, and optional traditional spices like ground cloves, anise seeds, or grains of Selim.
  • Oil: Neutral vegetable oil for deep-frying.

STEPS

  1. Prep the Plantains: Peel the plantains and cut them into small, bite-sized cubes or diagonal chunks.
  2. Make the Spice Paste: Blend the onion, ginger, peppers, and salt into a smooth paste using as little water as possible.
  3. Marinate: In a large bowl, toss the plantain cubes with the spice paste until they are thoroughly coated. Let them rest for 15–30 minutes to allow the flavors to penetrate the fruit.
  4. Heat the Oil: Heat about an inch of oil in a large skillet or wok to approximately 350°F (175°C). The oil is ready when a piece of plantain sizzles immediately upon contact.
  5. Fry in Batches: Carefully add the plantains to the oil. Do not overcrowd the pan, as this can make them soggy. Fry for 4–7 minutes, stirring occasionally until they are golden brown and caramelized.
  6. Drain and Serve: Use a slotted spoon to transfer the kelewele to a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.

For the most authentic experience, serve your kelewele hot with a side of roasted peanuts.
